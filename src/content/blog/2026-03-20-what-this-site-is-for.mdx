# What This Site Is For

Most personal sites drift toward one of two problems:

1. They become a resume with nicer typography.
2. They become a feed of half-finished thoughts.

I want this one to stay more practical than that.

The point of this site is simple:

- Keep a clear record of projects that were actually built.
- Write only when there is something concrete to explain.
- Prefer useful notes over performative opinions.

That is why the site is organized around two main sections.

## Projects

Projects are the center of gravity here. I want the work to be easy to browse, easy to understand, and easy to verify. If something is listed, it should point to a real library, app, API, or codebase.

## Blog

The blog is for lessons that survived contact with real work. Architecture decisions, implementation notes, tradeoffs, failures, and the occasional opinion when it is grounded in experience.

## Constraints

I like sites that are fast, clear, and calm. That means:

- minimal client-side JavaScript,
- strong typography,
- content-first navigation,
- and no decorative complexity that gets in the way of reading.

If this site does its job well, it should make it easier to understand what I build and how I think about software.
