I rebuilt this site because the old version was heavier than the problem required.

It worked, but it carried more framework surface area, more styling drift, and more client-side behavior than a content-first portfolio really needed. The site is mostly writing and project pages, so the right question was not _what can this stack do_, but _what is the minimum needed to ship a fast, maintainable result_.

## Why Astro

Astro fits this kind of site well:

- static output by default,
- MDX content collections,
- straightforward routing,
- and very little JavaScript unless I choose to add it.

That let me keep the focus on the actual content instead of spending time maintaining app-like structure for pages that are fundamentally documents.

## What Changed

The new site is intentionally narrower in scope.

- The blog now lives at `/blog`.
- Projects remain a first-class section at `/projects`.
- The old `/uses` page is gone.
- The design system is simpler and the theme handling is lighter.

I also wanted the visual tone to feel quieter and more editorial without becoming sterile.

## What I Want From It

The standard is simple:

- pages should load quickly,
- content should be readable on a phone or a laptop,
- project pages should make the work legible,
- and the site should be easy to evolve without a full rewrite every few months.

That is the main reason for the migration. Less ceremony, less drag, and a better place to keep the work.
